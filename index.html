History of the Colossi

The Shepherd of Primeval Trees would walk the land, cultivating forestation and caring for woodland beings for near a thousand years. In thanks to his mighty efforts, the Earth-Mother would bless him with the ability to awaken others like himself, should their destiny warrant.

Legends tell of the Shepherd appearing before logging camps, or the sites of great fires, deterring those who would seek to harm the ancient woods of the realm and restoring peace and prosperity to the folk who live within them.

Eventually, continually threatened by enclosing neighbouring settlements and marauding bands of Orcs and Fey, the Shepherd of Trees would raise his mighty fortress from the earth, and walk it across the Plane, carried by hundreds of Dymondwood Colossi.

But anger and jealousy is not so easily escaped.

The Dark Fey hungered for both the Shepherd's power, and his closeness to Gaia, and they would relentlessly seek to destroy him. They came with sharpened axes, wicked magi hurling fire, and siege weapons, but they were no match for the might of the Colossi. The Dark Fey searched the realms for something to help them, and they found it. They enlisted an ally that would change the fate of druids forever, Rhalstigar the Red Dragon.

Rhalstigar would earn his nickname that day, as the Incinerator swept the skies and left all beneath him blackened ash before his powerful breath of flames.

The pain of loss was too great to bear, and the Shepherd of Primaeval Trees would succumb to his agony. It is said Gaia wept for seven days and as many nights, conjuring a great storm that encompassed the realm, born of her child's passing.
